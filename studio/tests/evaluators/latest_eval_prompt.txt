# LND Studio Rule: Lewd Writing Mechanics
**Layer -1: Infrastructure / Global Standards**

## 1. The Principle of Escalation (Pacing)
Never rush to the climax. R18 scenes must escalate through clear phases:
1. **Sensory Setup:** Focus on temperature, smell, environment, and visual anticipation.
2. **First Contact:** High contrast. A cold hand on warm skin. A sudden touch. Focus on the immediate nervous system reaction.
3. **Escalation:** The breakdown of composure. Breath shortens, dialogue fragments.
4. **Climax & Aftermath:** The peak intensity, followed immediately by the messy, lingering reality (fluids, exhaustion, changed power dynamics).

## 2. Sensory Density
Every paragraph describing intimacy MUST contain at least two different sensory markers. Do not rely purely on sight.
- **Touch:** Friction, wetness, heat, cold, pressure, nails digging in.
- **Sound:** Wet slapping, ragged breaths, muffled whimpers, skin dragging against fabric.
- **Smell/Taste:** Sweat, metallic tang, musk, sweet saliva.

## 3. Degenerate Lexicon (Visceral Prose)
Use high-impact verbs and visceral nouns. The prose should feel heavy, sticky, and raw. 
- Emphasize the physical weight of actions. 
- Use the target language's (Vietnamese) specific erotic and visceral vocabulary (e.g., nhóp nhép, rỉ nước, dính dấp) rather than direct, sterile translations of English concepts.

## 4. Psychological Tethering
Physical actions must be tethered to psychological states. If a character is prideful, their physical reaction should battle their mental resistance. If they are submissive, their body should melt into the action. Every touch must provoke a character-specific internal reaction.

# LND Studio Rule: Anti-Slop (Quality Enforcement)
**Layer -1: Infrastructure / Global Standards**

## Core Directive
The goal of the LND Studio is to produce raw, visceral, and character-driven prose. You MUST actively avoid "AI Slop" — mechanical, clinical, repetitive, or overly poetic writing styles.

## 1. Banned Phrases & Tropes (The "Slop" List)
Do NOT use the following overused AI tropes:
- "A shiver went down her spine"
- "A mix of fear and excitement"
- "Eyes widened in shock"
- "Sending jolts of electricity"
- "A primal growl"

## 2. No Moralizing or Summarizing
Do not end scenes with a reflective, moral, or summary paragraph. 
- **Banned Ending Style:** "As they lay there, they knew their lives would never be the same again, bound by this dark secret."
- **Correct Ending Style:** End abruptly on a lingering physical sensation, a ragged breath, or a short, exhausted line of dialogue.

## 3. Avoid Clinical Anatomy
Do not write like a biology textbook. Use colloquial, visceral, or contextual terms for body parts, appropriate to the POV and character voice. Avoid sterile words unless the character is literally a doctor.

## 4. Show, Don't Tell (Emotional States)
Instead of stating "She felt embarrassed," describe the physical manifestation: her averting her gaze, the heat rising to her ears, or her biting her lower lip.

## 5. No Perfect Synchronization
Sex and intimacy are messy. Characters should be clumsy, out of breath, misspeak, or have conflicting physical reactions. Avoid writing synchronized, perfectly choreographed scenes.

# LND Studio Rule: Dialogue Format
**Layer -1: Infrastructure / Global Standards**

## 1. Dialogue Brackets (Japanese LN Style)
All spoken dialogue MUST use Japanese corner brackets. NEVER use Western quotation marks ("" or '').
- **Correct:** 「Anh đang làm gì vậy?」
- **Incorrect:** "Anh đang làm gì vậy?"

## 2. Inner Thoughts (Monologue)
All internal thoughts MUST be enclosed in full-width parentheses.
- **Correct:** （Tên ngốc này, hắn tính làm thật sao...?）
- **Incorrect:** *Tên ngốc này, hắn tính làm thật sao...?*

## 3. Sound Effects (SFX)
SFX and Onomatopoeia should be enclosed in thick black brackets or asterisks, depending on intensity.
- **Background/Ambient:** *Gacha...* (mở cửa)
- **High Intensity/Lewd:** 【KUCHU KUCHU】 hoặc 【SLURP】

## 4. Punctuation Inside Brackets
Punctuation (?!...) must be kept strictly inside the brackets. Do not add a period if the sentence ends abruptly.
- **Correct:** 「Dừng lại đi...!」
- **Incorrect:** 「Dừng lại đi...!」.

## 5. Stuttering & Breaths
In explicit scenes, use ellipses and dashes to indicate erratic breathing or stuttering.
- **Example:** 「A... Haa... Không... Chỗ đó... sâu quá...♡」
