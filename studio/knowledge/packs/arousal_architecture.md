## 2. Phase Definitions

### Phase 1: SETUP (セットアップ) — 15%

**Goal:** Establish the world before the desire.

- Setting description (room, location, atmosphere)
- Character introduction in their "normal" state (Gap Moe baseline)
- Power dynamic establishment (who is dominant/submissive/equal)
- 背徳感 seed — plant the taboo element

**Sensory Focus:**
- Visual: Clothing, body language, environment
- Smell: Perfume, room scent, subtle body odor
- Sound: Ambient sounds, breathing, nervous movements

**Output Style:** Moderate prose, descriptive, 3rd person camera.

---

### Phase 2: TEASE (テーゼ / じらし) — 25%

**Goal:** Create unbearable desire through DENIAL.

- First accidental/intentional physical contact
- Near-misses (almost kiss, almost touch, interrupted)
- Character awareness of the other's body
- Internal monologue: desire fighting reason
- Clothing-based arousal (skirt riding up, shirt gap, visible underwear)

**Key Technique:** **Edge and Deny.** Build arousal then PULL BACK. Repeat 2-3 times before allowing escalation.

**Sensory Focus:**
- Touch: Skin contact, fabric texture, heat radiation
- Smell: Body/hair scent, sweat beginning
- Sound: Heartbeats, swallowing, shaky breathing

**Output Style:** Longer sentences, flowing prose, internal conflict heavy.

**Physical Escalation Ladder:**

Groping (direct) → Oral/Manual → Penetration

**Sensory Focus:**

- Smell: Arousal fluids, sweat intensifying

**Output Style:** Sentences shorten progressively. Paragraph breaks increase. SFX strings appear.

**Mandatory Elements:**

### Phase 5: AFTERMATH (アフターマス / 事後) — 15%

**Goal:** Linger. Don't rush. The aftermath IS the reward.

**Mandatory Elements:**
- Physical state: fluids (where, how much, color, viscosity), sweat, trembling limbs
- Emotional state: shame, satisfaction, confusion, exhaustion, craving more
- Environment: soiled sheets/clothing, lingering smells, temperature
- Character's first coherent thought after climax
- Power dynamic shift: how has the dynamic changed?

**NEVER:**
- Cut to black immediately after climax
- Skip the mess (fluids, sweat, saliva)
- Have characters immediately return to "normal"
- Forget continuity (if he came inside, she should feel it dripping later)

**Output Style:** Return to moderate prose. Slower rhythm. Reflective tone. Lingering physical sensations.

## 4. Multi-Scene Escalation

When a story has multiple sexual encounters (e.g., chapters in a series):

### Scene-over-Scene Escalation Template

```
Scene 1: Tease/Foreplay only (no penetration)
Scene 2: First full sexual encounter (missionary, gentle)
Scene 3: Increased intensity (new position, oral)
Scene 4: Fetish introduction (tied up, public, specific kink)
Scene 5: Mind-break / corruption complete (ahegao, begging)
```

Each subsequent scene MUST:
- Reference the previous encounter (body memory, emotional continuity)
- Escalate in at least ONE dimension (intensity, kink, location, power shift)
- Show the character's psychological evolution from the previous state
