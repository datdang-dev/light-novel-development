## 2. Satisfaction Mechanics

### 2.1 What Makes a Scene "Satisfying" vs "Just Explicit"

| Factor | "Just Explicit" (退屈) | "Satisfying" (満足) |
|--------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Build-up | None or rushed | 60% of scene, unbearable tension |
| Character | Generic "beautiful girl" | Unique personality with gap moe |
| Taboo | Absent | Central to the arousal engine |
| Climax | Sudden, described mechanically | Earned, viscerally overwhelming |
| Aftermath | Cut to black | Messy, exhausted, emotionally revealing |
| Dialogue | "Iku!" only | Personality-consistent degradation/desperation |

### 2.2 The 4 Satisfaction Pillars (満足の4本柱)

1. **共感 (Kyōkan / Empathy):** Reader must understand character's desire
2. **期待 (Kitai / Anticipation):** Reader must WANT what's about to happen
3. **達成 (Tassei / Achievement):** The payoff must exceed expectation
4. **余韻 (Yoin / Afterglow):** Scene must linger in the reader's mind

## 5. 序破急 (Jo-Ha-Kyū) for Ero-Scenes

The Japanese 3-act structure adapted for R18 pacing:

### 序 (Jo / Introduction) — 30% of scene
- Establish setting, character state, and power dynamic
- Create the 背徳感 — why is this situation "wrong"?
- First non-sexual physical contact (hand touch, breath on neck)
- Character's INTERNAL conflict begins

### 破 (Ha / Development) — 50% of scene
- Physical escalation through stages:
  - Clothing loosening/removal
  - First genital contact
  - Oral / manual stimulation
  - Penetration
- EACH stage has its own mini-resistance → surrender cycle
- SFX density increases progressively
- Dialogue degrades from coherent → fragmented → animalistic

### 急 (Kyū / Rapid Climax) — 20% of scene
- STACCATO sentences. Short. Punchy. No complex grammar.
- Maximum SFX density
- Mind-break moment (if applicable)
- Physical climax described with full Fluid Viscosity Protocol
- Immediate aftermath: fluids, twitching, heavy breathing, mental state
